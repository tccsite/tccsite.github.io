会員制

地域教会の会員の規約を決めるにあたって、聖書による教会に関しての理解から始めるべきであると考える。

公同の教会

  1. 教会は神のものである。人間のものではないし、人間がつくり、建て上げるものでもない。完全に神の働きであり、神のものである。
    Matt 16:18; 1 Pet 2:9-10

  2. 教会は人である
    “教会”という言葉は“から呼ばれた”　または、“集まる”という意味がある。
    教会は神が人をこの世から神ご自身のものとなるように召されている。 1 Thess 2:12
    教会は神が終わりの日に地上からご自身のものとして、収穫し集められる。 Luke 10:2; John 4:35-36; 1 Thess 4:16-18
    教会は明らかに、人々をさす。建物や組織ではない。

  3. 教会は一つである。 聖書的には教会は一つしか存在しない。
    Rom 12:5, 1 Cor 10:17 & 12:12-13,20, Eph 2:16, 3:6, 4:4, 25 Col 3:15

  4. 教会は普遍的である。　イエスにある全ての信者は、どこにいても、どんな人種でも、ユダヤ人でも異邦人も含まれる。
    Gal 3:26-28; 1 Thess 4:16-17

  5. 教会はキリストの体であると書かれている。
    体の各器官は必要不可欠なつながりがある。統一性と相互依存性を伴う多様性がある。
    1 Cor 12:12-27

  6. 教会は聖徒である。　　教会はイエス・キリストと個人的な関係をもっている聖徒で構成されている。
    John 10:14, 14:20; 1 Cor 8:3; 2 Tim 2:19

  7. 教会は神の民である。 聖徒により構成された一つの普遍的な体である。

地域教会

  1. しかし、神がこの世から神ご自身のものとなるように召された、一つの普遍的な聖徒の体は、同時に地域のグループ、または体に集まり、それを地域教会と呼ぶ。

    これらの地域教会は人数の成長に伴い、構造と組織が必要となる。 (ex: Acts 6:1-7).
    今日の、様々な教派を見ると、それぞれ異なった構造や組織が存在することがわかる。
    神が地域教会に一つの特定の形を指定してないことを理解することは重要である。

    世界の様々な文化に福音が広がっていく時、この教会の柔軟性は重要である。
    様々な構造や組織は人間により作り上げられているので、弱さも持ち合わせている。
    しかし、神が中心であり、キリストが真に地域教会の頭（かしら）であるなら、どのような構造であても、その地域教会に集う聖徒にとって祝福となりうる。

    地域教会にとって、キリストへの従順であること、福音を述べ伝えること、愛することが鍵であり、構造ではない。

3. 会員制

人が救われた時、同時に普遍的なキリストの体に属するが、地域教会の会員になることは別である。
地域教会の会員になっても、本当に救われていない可能性はありうる。
また、聖徒であり、普遍的教会に属する忠実なものが、一時的に地域教会に属さないでいることもありうる。
ここでの“会員制”という言葉は地域教会の“会員”のみを意味している。
It is an important organizational step to protect & strengthen a local church as it grows.
It signifies a spiritual commitment between the individual and the local church.
The church commits to help care for the individual’s needs.
The individual commits to active involvement in the church through the following ways:

  1. Attending worship services and events
  2. Participating in the life of the church (ex: leading worship, teaching kids’ CS, youth ministry, annual business meetings, church representatives, cleaning, etc.)
  3. Tithing
  4. Praying
  5. Submitting to the authority, given by God to the local church leadership. Heb 13:17

(This will be explained more under #6 DISCIPLINE)
The ultimate purpose of membership in the local church is to serve & give glory to God in unity.
Rom 15:5-6

4. ACTIVE MEMBERSHIP RENEWAL

TCCh’s policy of membership is best described as: Active Membership Renewal. Active is the 5 ways the individual becomes involved in the local church (3. MEMBERSHIP above). Membership is the spiritual commitment between the individual and the local church (3. MEMBERSHIP). Renewal means that the commitment made for membership is renewed on a yearly basis. This is a simple process of verbal or written confirmation of one’s membership.

Why is a yearly renewal necessary?

  1. Renewal is a healthy reminder of our commitment to the local church.
  2. Renewal emphasizes the idea of being an actively involved member.
  3. Renewal gives membership greater meaning and significance.
  4. Renewal encourages local involvement wherever a person may live.
    Example: Believers who move away from TCCh will be encouraged to find a new local church
    and become an active member there instead of depending on a never expiring membership at TCCh.
  5. Renewal protects the local church.
    Example: when big decisions need to be made by the local church, people who are not active members in the local church will not be able to influence the decision process.
  6. Renewal helps the local church live by faith.
    Example: If a family moves to another city it will probably affect the tithes of TCCh. However, we want members of the body of Christ to be active where they live. TCCh wants to encourage living by faith in finances and all other areas of life.

5. REQUIREMENTS FOR MEMBERSHIP

  1. Commitment to Jesus Christ as personal Lord and Savior with a confession of faith.
  2. Baptism
  3. Commitment to the Word of God as authoritative for one’s Christian life.
  4. Commitment to the body life of TCCh.
  5. Commitment to the Statement of Faith and Policies of TCCh.
Once these requirements are met, an individual will be publically announced as a new member and lovingly welcomed by the other church members.

6. MEMBERSHIP STATUS DESIGNATIONS

The TCCh will have two types of membership: Active and Associate.
TCCh will allow dual membership under special conditions.
TCCh will also keep a “seki” 籍; a registration of baptism, for its members.

ACTIVE MEMBERSHIP
Active members are people who meet the membership requirements and make a membership commitment.
Active members can serve in ministry and also have voting rights on church decisions.

ASSOCIATE MEMBERSHIP
People who are unable to fulfill the description for being an active member, but still want to maintain a close relationship with TCCh can be granted an Associate Member status. Consider the following situations:

  1. A person who, because of work or schooling, is away from Kagawa for an extended period of time but comes back to visit and will eventually return.
  2. A person who moves away but wants to stay connected temporarily with TCCh until they find a new church to join in their new location.
  3. A person who wants to keep their main membership in a different “home church” (ex: in the Philippines) yet still wants to be actively involved in TCCh.
Associate members still need to meet the requirements for membership.
Associate members can serve in ministry but will not have voting rights on church decisions.

DUAL MEMBERSHIP

How does my Active/Associate membership with TCCh relate to my membership in another church?　　
The TCCh will allow dual memberships when the three following things are true:

  • If the member truly lives in two different locations or countries.
  • If there is a high probability of returning to the original church in the near future.
    • If both churches embrace dual membership or some related affiliation.

    SEKI (籍): REGISTRATION OF BAPTISM

    Explanation of the Japanese “seki”
    For TCCh, the Japanese idea of “seki” (籍) will refer to a person’s registration of baptism only and not to a person’s membership status (most Japanese churches combine the two). When a person is saved and baptized in the Takamatsu Community Church they will have their “seki” 籍 (registration of Baptism) with the TCCh. However seki is different from their membership. For example: if a person is not living in Kagawa and they are no longer a member here, their seki can still remain here as long as they wish. People can transfer their seki from one church to TCCh after talking with the leadership. The TCCh leaders will also gladly help in any transition of seki from TCCh to another church.

    7. DISCIPLINE IN THE LOCAL CHURCH

    As mentioned in the explanation of membership (Section 3 point#5) church discipline is a policy of the TCCh that all members agree to. In order to protect a local church from the influence of hypocrisy & false teaching and also to restore, through repentance, believers who have fallen into sin, a form of discipline is necessary in the local church. Several key guidelines for discipline in a local church are outlined in the Bible. A clear policy of local church membership is the foundation for a healthy policy of church　discipline. If a person consciously agrees to submit to the authority of the local church leadership when they become a member, then if there is a problem of sin, the leadership can follow the Biblical　principles to help restore this person who has fallen into sin. Without such a spiritual commitment, either sin will go unaddressed by the leadership or the authority of the leadership may not be respected resulting in many problems for the church. Discipline is an act of love in an attitude of humility with the goal of protecting truth, unifying the body, and restoring those fallen into sin.
    Heb 12:5-6; Gal 6:1-3
